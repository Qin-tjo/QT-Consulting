What I do

Three ways to work together

Most engagements start with a single build, then grow into keeping it and the space around it current. And if you are not sure what you need yet, we can figure that out together.

01

A dashboard built for your question Best entry point

A live, auditable view of a target, a competitor, or a whole space. Genomic landscape, mechanism, the clinical field, and a synthesis that respects evidence quality. Sourced line by line, shaped to your pipeline, and delivered however your team works: a live dashboard, an analysis package, or a slide-ready deck. I can build from open data, and from your own data when you have it.

For: biotech BD, strategy, and translational teams.
02

Living intelligence retainer The ongoing engagement

I keep your dashboard current and watch the space for you. A verified weekly brief on new trials, readouts, and papers for your target, with an update-and-verify loop that keeps it trustworthy over time, and my judgment on every update.

For: biotech ops & translational teams tracking a space.
03

Not sure what you need? Let's scope it

We can work through what would actually help your team and build it together. It does not have to be target or competitive intelligence — bring the question, and we'll shape the right deliverable around it.

For: any team with a question that deserves a scientist's answer.

About

Qin Tjokrosurjo, PhD

I'm a cancer biologist. Most recently Senior Scientist at Bicycle Therapeutics, leading biomarker and translational strategy across clinical-stage oncology programs. Before that I led CRISPR-based immunotherapy target screening at Harvard (co-inventor on a patent for novel targets) and earned my PhD in Cancer Biology at the University of Florida.

My work spans 11 peer-reviewed publications, including Nature Immunology, JEM, and Science Advances, across oncology, IBD, and diabetes. I've worked from lab experiments to computational analysis, and from basic science through to the clinic, so I can meet a program wherever it is and build what it needs.
